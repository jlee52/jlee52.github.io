Jeonghwan "Jay" Lee

I am a PhD student in Mechanical Engineering at The University of Texas at Austin. I have worked with James Sulzer at Rehabilitation with Insight from Robotics and Engineering (Rewire) Lab on developing technology that helps characterize the impaired sensorimotor system and transform that knowledge into a novel, more effective interventions after neurological injury.

Before joining a PhD program at UT Austin, I graduated with MSc in Mechanical Engineering at Seoul National University in South Korea, where I was advised by Kunwoo Lee. I did my BSc in Mechanical Engineering at Hanyang University in South Korea.

Research

I'm interested in Design Optimization, Biomechanics, Data Science, and Robotics. Much of my current work is about exploring how to optimize robotic exoskeletal assistance by characterizing pathological gait after neurological impairments, such as the post-stroke stiff-knee gait. Former studies were about developing an affordable robotic gait trainer, computer-assisted surgery, and medical image processing.
