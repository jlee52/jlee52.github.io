Jeonghwan 'Jay' Lee

I earned my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Texas at Austin in 2022, under the mentorship of Dr. James Sulzer. My doctoral research focused on optimizing the design of an affordable gait trainer, investigating key biomechanical features to prevent spastic responses during wearable exoskeletal assistance for post-stroke gait, and characterizing post-stroke gait patterns to enhance the accuracy and reliability of clinical diagnoses and assessments.

Since completing my Ph.D., I am working as a Senior Robotic Engineer at Contoro Robotics, where I lead the development of robot perception software to ensure safe and accurate task execution..

Research

I am interested in Data Science, Design Optimization, Biomechanics and Robotics. My Ph.D. works include characterizing pathological gait after neurological impairment by machine learning and exploring optimal design parameters to design a transparent rigid robotic gait trainer for post-stroke gait rehabilitation computationally and experimentally. Prior to this, I also contributed to projects related to computer-assisted surgery and medical image processing.
